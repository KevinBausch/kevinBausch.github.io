How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Knowing the principles of neuroplasticity allow us to understand the biological processes of our brain and how it works under different stimuli. This can provide us a powerful tool to improve our learning and memory.

  • Use it or lose it: our brain will delete the information or knowledge (neuronal conecctions) that are not used, so it is better to practice everything we learn to make the conections stronger everytime.

  • Use it and improve it: the more we use the new knowledge the better and stronger the conections will become in our brain, so practice is the key.

  • Specificity: The connection can become stronger when we do the same thing in different ways, the learning will be solid.

  • Repetition matters: The more time we spend practicing and repeating something the better will be our performance.

  • Intensity: challenges allow learning

  • Time: There is a specific moments when we canc reate better connections, some people are morning persons and others night persons.

  • Saliences: Our emotions are key to create connections, the more motivated we are the easier to learn something will be.

  • Age: our brains are flexible independent of our age but is better to be mentally active.

  • Transference: we can use the same way of learning to different topics

  • Interference: Our brain just keep learning the whole time without filtering good or bad habits, that is why it is important to have good learning routines sincethe beginning.

How you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit?

From a personal point of view I think I have been practising some of them without knowing them in depth but now that I have a better understanding of these neuroplasticity principles I think I have to keep my motivation up with small challenges, this will keep me repeating and practicing activities until the learning is solid and the neuronal connections are strong. I have notice that I really need to follow structures and it is better for me to create a schedule or a daily learning plan to follow step by step to avoid creating bad habits.

Growth mindset

What it is and why it is relevant?

It is important because it helps us to overcome difficult situations at the moment of learning allowing us to develop new skills. It opens new learning possibilities changing our perception.


In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Yes, I have noticed how important is to understand the principles of neuroplasticity overall about motivation and interference. Now I think I am ore aware to stop bad learning habits.


How will you integrate a growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will adopt a view where challenges are a gift, an opportunity to evolve and develop new skills. I understand that learning is a long process and requires practice, motivation and determination to succeed.

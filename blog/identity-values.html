Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I did not have ethical decisions to make since I always based my decisions on my thoughts or personal values and not on my emotions. Of course, I have been in situations where you could feel trapped in a dilemma because you need to help a friend or family but if you take out your feeling or emotions from the equation then every situation will become easier to resolve.
Also if your social circle share the same values as you then you speak the same code there is no reason to be trapped in a dilemma where you have to choose between two options because there is only one, the correct one. I think the idea is to make decisions that will make you feel proud of yourself. That is why I consider respect, honour and loyalty as my values.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I think my culture influenced my personal values and identity since it was clear for me to see the contrast between what is correct and what is not correct. In Peru, where I come from you can see, from my personal perspective, a huge contrast in terms of values as a society.
In some places, you will find friendly and honest people eager to help others and cause a positive impact in the world but at the same time, you can see people who are only focused on themselves that they can be so inconsiderate with other human beings in their everyday actions.
